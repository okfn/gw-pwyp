Ending Secrecy
Why New Transparency Rules Matter
   
Picture a community in a developing country, rich in natural resources...
...where half of the inhabitants live on under US $2/day, many have no access to clean water; mothers die unnecessarily in childbirth, and most children don't go to school.
When natural resources worth millions (or billions) are discovered near this community, this should change, right?
Natural resources are sold off to foreign companies while local communities cannot see who is doing the deals, how they're being done, or who is benefiting.
Conditions remain the same.
If companies make millions in profit and government revenues increase so dramatically, why is there no change?
Unaccountable elites keep communities in the dark about their payments and revenues, which helps keep them in abject poverty.

Secrecy makes it easier for government officials to embezzle funds instead of using them to improve social and economic conditions.

It makes it easier for oil, gas and mining companies to turn a blind eye and ignore this problem.

Laws such as Section 1504 of the US Dodd-Frank Act and similar legislation in the EU outlaw this kind of secrecy by requiring oil, gas and mining companies to publish the payments they make to governments. Shining a light on these massive payments will allow communities to ask questions and hold governments and companies accountable so that natural resources can benefit everybody.
